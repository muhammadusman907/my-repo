The following table is an illustration of the different types of suspension scaffolds:

Type of Suspension Scaffold

Image

Single-point adjustable suspension scaffold:

This type of scaffold is commonly utilized by window washers for exterior building cleaning.

Two-point adjustable suspension scaffold:

This type of scaffold is commonly referred to as a swing-stage scaffold and is widely used across industries by window washers on skyscrapers and extensively utilized in high-rise construction. These scaffolds are suspended by ropes or cables connected to stirrups at each end of the platform.

Multi-point adjustable scaffold: This type of scaffold has one or more platforms suspended by more than two ropes from overhead supports and is equipped with a means to permit the platform to be raised and lowered. Includes chimney hoists.

Catenary scaffold: A catenary scaffold is a scaffold consisting of a platform supported by two essentially horizontal and parallel ropes attached to structural members of a building or other structure. Additional vertical pickups may also provide support.

Float or ship scaffold: This type of scaffold uses a braced platform resting on two parallel bearers. It is hung from overhead supports by ropes of fixed length.

Interior hung scaffold: This type of scaffold has a platform that is suspended from the ceiling or roof structure by fixed-length supports.

Multi-level scaffold: This is a two-point or multi-point adjustable suspension scaffold with a series of platforms at various levels resting on common stirrups.

Needle beam scaffold: This type of suspended scaffold consists of a platform suspended from needle beams.
